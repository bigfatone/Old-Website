User Experience of Final Fantasy XIV
ROI Presentation

Why Customer Service?

Square Enix needs a stronger effort in customer service to retain more loyal subscribers

Customers lost to poor customer service is losing the company in almost a million in revenue per month

Customer service representatives tend to be the first to learn about why they are losing otherwise loyal customers

What's wrong with it?

Square Enix customer support is perceived as a negative experience by customers voicing complaints on the official forums and related message boards

Customers spend too much time waiting for assistance

Customer service does not contour itself according to the customer's unique situation

Customer Service Example:

Final Fantasy XIV: A Realm Reborn

Estimated Financial Losses Due to Poor Customer Service

One entry level monthly subscription Final Fantasy XIV is equal to $13 US Dollars.

As of June 2017, there are over 108 million subscriber accounts worldwide. Out of these 108 million, 465,000 are subscribed on a month to month basis.

Between May and June, roughly 60,000 subscribers did not retain their monthly subscriptions. This means if Square Enix can improve subscriber retention, they can recoup up to $780,000 USD in monthly subscription revenue.

Customer Support Journey

Key Points

Time spent for both phone and webchat support methods can take up to two hours to resolve the customer's problem.

There is a high risk of denying the customer any support without identification. This can happen for various reasons such as forgetting the answers to security questions. If the customer cannot verify their identity they must submit a notarized letter to Square Enix stating that they are the real owner of the account in question.

Addressing all three methods of support (phone, online chat, and email) will greatly reduce the time it takes to deal with the customer's problem and move on with their gaming experience.

Potential Solutions

Improved automated solutions database will reduce time spent by 90%. Lowering hold times for customers will reduce time spent by 80%. Quicker email-based assistance will reduce time spent by 83%.

More time saved means more time used to help other customers, and more customers can return to enjoying their game.

Solutions Are Not Mutually Exlusive

Better automated solutions can completely remove the customer from requiring assistance and cut down on hold times, and improved email solutions can remove confusion and streamline the customer support process.
